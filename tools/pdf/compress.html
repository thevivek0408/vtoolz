Compress PDF — Frequently Asked Questions

How does PDF compression work?

Vibox compresses PDFs using PDF-lib, a browser-native library. It removes redundant data, compresses embedded images at your chosen quality level, and strips unnecessary metadata. The entire process runs in your browser — no file uploads, no server required.

Do my PDFs get uploaded to a server?

No. All compression happens locally in your browser. Your PDF never leaves your device. This is especially important for sensitive documents such as contracts, invoices, tax returns, or medical records. Unlike SmallPDF, iLovePDF, or Adobe's online tools, Vibox never transmits your files to any server.

How much can a PDF be compressed?

Results depend on the PDF content. PDFs containing high-resolution photos can be reduced by 50–80%. PDFs with mostly text and vector graphics typically compress less (5–20%) because text is already compact. Scanned PDFs with large uncompressed image layers usually compress the most.

What do the Low, Medium, and High presets mean?

The presets control how aggressively images inside the PDF are resampled. High compression reduces file size the most but may slightly reduce image sharpness. Medium is the best all-around balance. Low preserves near-original quality while still removing unnecessary metadata and redundant data structures.

Will text still be readable after compression?

Yes. Text in PDFs is stored as vector data and is not affected by image compression settings. Only rasterised images within the document are resampled. All text, fonts, and vector graphics remain fully sharp and readable after compression at any preset.

Is PDF compression free?

Yes. Vibox PDF compression is 100% free with no watermarks on output, no file size limits, and no account required. You can compress as many PDFs as you like, as often as needed, at no cost.

Can I compress a PDF on my phone?

Yes. The tool works on any modern mobile browser. Open vibox.app on your phone, go to the PDF Compressor, and upload your PDF from your Files app or cloud storage. Compression runs on your device and the smaller file downloads directly to your phone.
