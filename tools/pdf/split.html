Split PDF — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I split a PDF file?

Upload your PDF by dragging it onto the area or clicking to browse. Vibox displays a visual thumbnail preview of every page. Click the pages you want to extract — they highlight when selected. Then click Extract Selected Pages and your new PDF downloads instantly.

Do my PDFs get uploaded to a server?

No. Vibox processes your PDF entirely in your browser using PDF-lib and PDF.js. Your document is never sent to any server. This is important for documents containing personal data, legal content, or financial information that must stay private.

Can I extract multiple non-consecutive pages?

Yes. You can select any combination of pages — they do not need to be consecutive. Click each thumbnail to select or deselect. All selected pages are extracted into a single new PDF in the order they appear in the original document.

Can I split a PDF into individual single-page files?

Currently the tool extracts your page selection into a single PDF. To create separate single-page PDFs, extract each page individually. You can also use the Delete PDF Pages tool to remove pages you do not need.

Is PDF splitting free?

Yes. Splitting PDFs with Vibox is completely free. No account needed, no watermarks on the output, and no file size limits. Unlike services that restrict splitting to paid subscribers, Vibox provides this feature free forever.

Will the extracted pages keep their original quality?

Yes. Page splitting does not recompress or alter any page content. Pages are copied directly from the original PDF, so text, images, and vector graphics are preserved at full fidelity in the output document.

How large a PDF can I split?

There is no server-side size limit. The limit depends on your device's available memory. Most devices handle PDFs up to 100 MB comfortably. Very large PDFs with many high-resolution scanned pages may take a few extra seconds to load the page previews.
